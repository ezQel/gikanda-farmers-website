What We Grow

We grow Arabica Coffee, where there are several categories

SL28 (Scott Agricultural Laboratories 28)

SL28 is a popular coffee variety in Kenya and is known for its exceptional cup quality. It is prized for its bright acidity, full body, and complex flavors, often characterized by fruity and floral notes. The variety is suited for medium to high altitudes and shows resistance to drought, but is susceptible to the major diseases of coffee. SL28 is notable for its rusticity—a quality meaning that it can be left untended for years or even decades at a time, and then return to successful production.

SL34 (Scott Agricultural Laboratories 34)

Similar to SL28, SL34 is another Kenyan coffee variety. It is recognized for its good disease resistance and is favored for its bright acidity and rich, full-bodied flavor profile. SL34 is adapted to high altitude areas with good rainfall. It is characterized by dark bronze shoot tipped plants with a few green-tipped strains. The laterals have semi-erect habit which tend to droop on older primary branches. It was bred in Scott Agricultural Laboratories

Batian

Batian is a relatively newer coffee variety in Kenya, developed as a disease-resistant and drought-tolerant coffee plant. It was created to address the challenges posed by coffee leaf rust (CLR). Batian coffee often produces a cup with bright acidity and a clean, crisp taste. Batian is a composite variety, mixing three different pure line varieties. The varieties involved in the original crosses are: SL28,SL34 Rume Sudan, N39, K7, SL4 and the Timor Hybrid. Well-adapted for smallholders because of its rare combination of being a tall variety with disease resistance and resillience (e.g., can cope with low management and adverse environmental conditions). Bred by Coffee Research Foundation (now Kenya Agricultural and Livestock Research Organization, KALRO)

Ruiru 11

Ruiru 11 is another disease-resistant coffee variety, designed to withstand common coffee diseases. It has gained popularity in Kenya for its ability to yield good-quality coffee while being hardy against pests and diseases. To mass propagate Ruiru 11, breeders manually pollinate the mother plants with pollen from the male parent lines to produce first-generation (F1) hybrid seed. This final cross results in hybrid vigor, hence high yield. The resulting seeds produce a set of siblings that are mixed together and distributed to farmers. This is called a composite variety.Because Ruiru 11 relies on hand pollination for mass propagation, it has been difficult to produce adequate volumes of seed to meet farmer demand.

K7

This coffee variety is known for its resistance to coffee berry disease (CBD). It is cultivated in some parts of Kenya, including the Mount Kenya region K7 is distinguished by its spreading habit on young laterals although older primaries tend to droop. It has characteristic medium to narrow leaves with young shoot-tips that are light bronze in color. It is suited for lower altitudes where coffee leaf rust is prevalent.
